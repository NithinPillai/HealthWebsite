Method of Use

Smoked through electric devices that can be bought at local convenience stores and gas stations.

Physical Effects

Short term effects: coughing, dry mouth and throat, shortness of breath, mouth and throat irritation, headaches, Weakened taste, Sleepiness. Long term effects: severe lung injuries, seizures, nicotine addiction and poisoning, increased risk of heart attacks and strokes (long term effects have not been established).

Mental Effects

Anxiety, suicidal tendencies, and clinical depression.

Vapers have 2x chance of becoming clinically depressed.

Drug Schedule

The Controlled Substances Act passed in 1970, organizes narcotic substances into five categories, or "schedules." Drugs are sorted into their schedules based on their potential for abuse, legal status, and medicinal uses. Schedule I is for the drugs with the highest dangers, and Schedule V is for narcotics with a relatively low potential for addiction, but E-Cigarettes are not included in these schedules despite their clear health implications. In turn E-Cigarettes have their own dangers due to legal leniency and lack of concern.
